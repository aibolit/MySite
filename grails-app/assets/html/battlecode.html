This is where a lot of it started. Battlecode is a month long AI programming competition at MIT. I made a player freshman year, but after that, I became a director. I got to design and build the game, the map editor, and a web viewer to display matches in the browser. More immportantly, it was the start of my passion of encouraging learning through playing games. I ran lectures organized, organized coding sessions, and worked with the teams to help develop their player.
Battlecode was very inspirational to me, so I couldn't just leave. When I started recruiting from MIT with Bloomberg, I made sure that Battlecode got a sponsorship, and that we could show what Bloomberg was all about.
In order to do this. I modified the engine to make a single-player version of the game with special objectives. One year, it was to fight off the zombies hordes (an overpowered AI), another year it was to navigate a complex maze.
