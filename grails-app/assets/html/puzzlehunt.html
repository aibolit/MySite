I built this website after we ran Bloomberg's first Mystery Hunt and realized we had a logistics issue. The people giving out the puzzle materials needed a quick way to see who had access to which puzzles and the thirty people in needed an efficient way to be assigned hint requests and communicates with the teams.
I built this website to manage eveything about the Mystery Hunt. It has logins for players, admins, hinters and the copy room, with the ability for eveyone to communicate.
  • Players can view their puzzles and rewards, submit answers and ask for hints.
  • Hinters get assigned requests based on many factors including puzzle difficulty, how long the request has been open, and whether they have helped the team before.
  • The copy room can tell which puzzles they can give out to whom.
  • Finally Admins can change anything about the hunt in real time.
One of the coolest properties is that all of the puzzles, players, rewards and even style of the website are defined by a single json config file. Nothing is hard-coded; the site is all ready for next year.

- java, groovy, grails
