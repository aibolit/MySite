My manager came to me. "Can you help me with recruiting", he said. "When I go to recruit from Virginia Tech, all the students come to us looking for banking jobs. Can you run one of those 'Battlecode' events to show the students we are a Tech and Media company?".
Two months later I had a game that was quite different. On the surface it looked like a stock exchange. The difference was that the only market changing actions could be taken by the players connected to it. There were a few other wacky rules, like earning exponentially decreasing dividends every second on what your player owned (to keep the trades moving at a faster pace).
The only way to play was to connect to an api we provided. We ran it at Virginia tech, and created a video. One of the students even blogged about it.
Since then, we have have run the event in universities across five states and Canada as overnight hackcathons. It'a a very rewarding feeling teaching network programming with a team of students, and then three hours later talking AI and strategy.
